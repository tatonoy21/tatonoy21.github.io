🏏 What is Cricket?

Cricket is a bat-and-ball sport played between two teams of eleven players. One team bats to score runs while the other bowls and fields to stop them. The team with the most runs wins.

Objectives

  • Score as many runs as possible.
  • Dismiss the opposing batters.
  • Protect your wicket.
  • Work together as a team.

🏏 How to Play Cricket

Step 1

A coin toss decides the order in which the teams bat and field. The two team captains contest the toss with the winning captain choosing whether to bat or bowl first

Step 2

Both teams take turns batting and fielding.

Step 3

Players on the batting side bat in pairs, with one situated at each end of the wicket

Step 4

Stumps are comprised of three upright wooden posts placed side by side in the ground, with two smaller sticks laid horizontally on top of the posts called bails to form the wicket

Step 5

Two batsmen from the batting team are on the field at a time. One faces the bowler to hit the ball, while the other waits at the opposite end. They score runs by running between the two wickets after hitting the ball or by hitting the ball over the boundary line.

Step 6

The bowler throws (bowls) the ball toward the batter's wicket to try to hit the stumps or force the batter into an error. The fielding team catches the ball or retrieves it quickly to prevent the batsmen from scoring.

Step 7

Twenty20 and one-day matches are formed of limited overs. As the name suggests, teams playing a Twenty20 game bat for a total of 20 overs each, while in one-day cricket, the total number of overs each side bats for is 50

step 8

An over consists of six balls (deliveries) by a bowler from the fielding team

Step 9

Teams bat until they have either completed their overs or ten of their batsmen have been dismissed by the fielding side

Step10

The team with the highest number of runs at the end wins!

📜 Basic Rules

Runs

Each completed run adds 1 point to the batting team.

Boundary Four

A ball reaching the boundary after bouncing scores 4 runs.

Six

A ball clearing the boundary without bouncing scores 6 runs.

Out

Players can be out by bowled, caught, run out, LBW, or stumped.

Overs

One over consists of 6 legal balls.

Winner

The team with the highest score wins the match.

🌍 Fielding Positions Explained

Slip

Placed behind the batter to catch edges.

Gully

Close to the batter and ready for quick catches.

Point

Stops sharp cuts and drives on the off side.

Cover

Protects the gap between point and mid-off.

Mid-off

Supports the bowler in the middle of the field.

Mid-on

Patrols the on-side and cuts off straight drives.

Square Leg

Important for catching pulls and hooks.

Fine Leg

Placed near the boundary for leg-side catches.

Third Man

Patrols the gap behind the batter on the off side.

Long Off

Deep fielder for big hits down the ground.

Long On

Deep fielder for strong hits on the leg side.
